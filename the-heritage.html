05. The Heritage

Honest wood.
Honest craft.

The Heritage is a declaration that beauty and integrity are found in authenticity, not ornamentation. Crafted from reclaimed Opepe harvested from decommissioned structures across Benue and Kogi States, every plank carries the honest marks of its previous life — grain, mineral streaks, and the occasional knot — left intact and celebrated.

The surface is finished with raw linseed oil, rubbed into the wood by hand over three sessions. The result is a piece that looks and feels like something passed down — deeply warm, rooted in the land, and built to honour a life fully lived. The interior is lined in natural undyed linen with Nigerian cotton filling. Simple. True. Dignified.

The Wood

Opepe: the
enduring one.

Opepe (Nauclea diderrichii) is one of the great hardwoods of West Africa. Native to the forests of Benue, Edo, and Cross River States, it is prized for its exceptional durability — historically used in bridge and railway sleeper construction, often outlasting the structures around it by decades.

The reclaimed Opepe used in The Heritage is sourced from structures being decommissioned across central Nigeria. In choosing this timber, we honour both the wood's long history and the principle that the finest materials need not come from a fresh cut. This Opepe has earned its character. We simply reveal it.
