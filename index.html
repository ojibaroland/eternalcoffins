Nigerian Materials

Sourced from the forests
of Nigeria.

Iroko (African Teak)
Sourced from Nigerian forests. Exceptionally dense and durable, with a warm golden-brown tone that deepens richly with age.
African Mahogany (Khaya)
Grown across southern Nigeria. Deep reddish-brown with a fine, even grain — the hallmark of West African luxury woodwork.
Sapele
Native to Nigeria and West Africa. Quartersawn Sapele reveals a rare interlocked ribbon-grain of extraordinary, shimmering beauty.
Afromosia
Nigeria's rarest hardwood, known as African Rosewood. Golden-brown heartwood with a fine, uniform grain of breathtaking refinement.

How We Work

A process built on
listening.

01
Consultation
A private conversation to understand the person you loved, your wishes, and your timeline. No forms. Just people.
02
Design
We work with you to select materials, dimensions, hardware, and any bespoke engravings or inlays.
03
Craft
Our master craftsmen build your piece entirely by hand — joinery, finishing, lining — over 3 to 10 working days.
04
Delivery
White-glove delivery directly to your chosen funeral home or family home, wrapped and handled with total care.
